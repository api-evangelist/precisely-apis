<p>Utilities invest billions modernizing infrastructure, improving reliability, and preparing for a more digital, data‑driven future. Yet one of the most persistent – and underestimated – sources of cost and operational risk often hides in plain sight:</p>
<p><strong>Bad address data.</strong></p>
<p>Across your customer systems, asset records, billing platforms, outage management, and regulatory reporting, inaccurate or incomplete addresses create inefficiencies every day. And for many utilities, the problem has been compounding for years.</p>
<h2>Why is Address Data Quality Such a Big Problem for Utilities?</h2>
<p>In our conversations with large North American utilities, a consistent pattern emerges:<br />
address data is everywhere – but <strong>owned nowhere</strong>.</p>
<p>The experiences that we hear often go something like this:</p>
<p>Customer Information Systems (CIS), GIS, AMI, billing, work management, and outage platforms each maintain their own version of an address.</p>
<p>Over time, those versions drift.</p>
<ul>
<li>Manual overrides become the norm.</li>
<li>Legacy systems persist untouched for a decade or more.</li>
<li>No single team owns accuracy end-to-end</li>
</ul>
<p>The result? Fragmentation that no one owns – but everyone pays for.</p>
<p>Utilities have shared that <strong>well over 10% of their address records contain issues</strong>, ranging from missing unit numbers to incorrect street data or mismatched premise identifiers. At scale, that translates into hundreds of thousands – or millions – of problematic records.</p>

<h2>The Real Cost of Bad Address Data in Utilities</h2>
<p>Address data issues are often dismissed as “data cleanup problems.” In reality, they directly drive measurable cost, risk, and customer friction.</p>
<p><strong>Return mail</strong> is the most visible example. Each undeliverable bill or notice costs several dollars once you factor in printing, postage, and handling. Even modest return rates can add up to <strong>millions in avoidable annual spend</strong> for large utilities.</p>
<p>But the bigger downstream impacts show up across your operations:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Truck rolls to the wrong location</strong>, wasting field resources and delaying service restoration</li>
<li><strong>Billing errors and disputes</strong> from incorrect service addresses or premise mismatches</li>
<li><strong>Delayed or failed outage communications</strong>, impacting SAIDI/SAIFI metrics and customer trust</li>
<li><strong>Regulatory and tax exposure</strong>, when incorrect addresses lead to misapplied jurisdictions or franchise fees</li>
<li><strong>Unreliable analytics and AI</strong>, because poor address data undermines every model built on top of it</li>
</ul>
<p>Here’s the challenge: you often don’t see these issues until the costs accumulate – or until modernization initiatives stall because your underlying data isn’t fit for purpose.</p>
<h2>Why Traditional Address Data Fixes Fall Short</h2>
<p>Most utilities already validate addresses somewhere in the process – but where?</p>
<p>In many utilities, address cleansing happens downstream, just before mail is printed or reports are generated.</p>
<p>This might improve deliverability in the moment, but it <strong>doesn’t fix the source of truth</strong>.</p>
<p>The same bad address continues to flow into:</p>
<ul>
<li>New customer onboarding</li>
<li>Work orders</li>
<li>Asset records</li>
<li>Outage systems</li>
<li>Analytics pipelines</li>
</ul>
<p>Without a persistent, shared definition of a premise and its location, utilities end up correcting the same errors over and over again – absorbing cost without closing the gap.</p>
<h2>How Can Utilities Improve Address Data Quality and Management?</h2>
<p>Precisely works with utilities to treat address data quality not as a one‑off cleanup exercise, but as <strong>foundational infrastructure</strong>.</p>
<p>That starts with standardizing and validating addresses <strong>at the point of entry</strong>, not just downstream. Addresses are corrected, enriched, and tied to a <strong>persistent location identifier</strong> that links customers, meters, assets, parcels, and jurisdictions across systems.</p>
<p>From there, you can:</p>
<ul>
<li>Reconcile CIS, GIS, and AMI records against a single trusted premise view</li>
<li>Detect and manage exceptions through stewardship workflows instead of manual rework</li>
<li>Accurately classify single‑family, multi‑unit, and commercial locations</li>
<li>Apply authoritative boundary data for taxation, reporting, and compliance</li>
<li>Support AI, analytics, and automation with reliable, location-based data</li>
</ul>
<p>The outcome is cleaner data – and critically,  <strong>fewer operational failures, lower costs, and higher confidence in every decision and downstream process</strong>.</p>
